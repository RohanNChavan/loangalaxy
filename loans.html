Eligibility & Important Factors

Eligibility is based on multiple factors. Understanding them helps improve approval chances and reduce delays.

1) Employment status

Employment type directly affects loan eligibility. Salaried applicants typically have stable monthly income and predictable cash flow, which can help in faster evaluation. Self-employed borrowers may be assessed on business stability, income history, and consistency of earnings. Students and unemployed individuals may require additional checks or co-applicant support depending on the loan provider.

2) Monthly income

Income is a key indicator for repayment capacity. Lenders usually check if your existing obligations (like EMIs, rent, credit card payments) still leave room for another EMI. A higher income can improve eligibility and can also impact available loan amount.

3) PAN details

PAN is required for identity and credit assessment. It allows verification and helps evaluate the credit behavior (like timely repayments). Always enter your PAN carefully in the correct format. Even a small mistake can cause application errors.

4) PIN code and location

Some loan providers may serve selected geographies. PIN code helps identify your serviceable area and ensures faster processing. A correct address PIN code reduces back-and-forth and delays.

5) Requested loan amount

Loan eligibility depends on the amount requested. If a customer requests a higher loan amount than their eligible limit, approvals can get delayed. We recommend applying with a realistic range based on monthly income.

Common reasons for delay or rejection

  • Incorrect PAN format or mismatch
  • Invalid phone number / unreachable applicant
  • High existing EMI burden vs income
  • Incorrect pincode / non-serviceable location
  • Multiple recent applications across platforms

Documents Typically Required

Exact requirements vary based on profile and lender. These are the most commonly requested documents.

Identity Proof

PAN card is required. Some providers also request Aadhaar for verification.

Income Proof

Salary slips / bank statements for salaried. ITR / bank statements for self-employed.

Address Proof

Utility bill / Aadhaar / rental agreement (depending on requirement and verification).

Simple tip to avoid errors

Keep documents ready and ensure details match — especially name spelling, PAN, and registered mobile number. This improves processing speed and reduces verification issues.
