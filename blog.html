Personal finance guidance (simple & practical)

Below is a complete guide to help you apply smarter and manage loans responsibly.

1) Why credit score matters (and what it really means)

A credit score is a summary of your repayment behavior. It reflects how consistently you repay EMIs, credit card bills, and other obligations. It does not depend on income alone — even someone with high income can have a low score if payments are delayed frequently.

A healthy credit score improves your chances of approval and may lead to better interest rates. However, it’s not the only factor. Lenders also look at income stability, employment type, existing EMIs, and overall financial profile.

Simple habits to maintain a strong credit score

  • Pay EMIs and credit card bills before due date
  • Avoid maxing out credit limit (keep utilisation low)
  • Don’t apply for too many loans/cards within a short time
  • Keep old accounts active (history matters)

2) Loan eligibility: what lenders usually evaluate

Many customers assume eligibility depends only on salary. In reality, lenders evaluate: income stability, existing obligations, repayment history, and ability to maintain EMI comfortably.

What improves eligibility?

  • Stable income and job/business continuity
  • Low EMI burden relative to income
  • Clean repayment history (no frequent missed payments)
  • Correct PAN and verified mobile number

3) Monthly income and EMI planning

EMI planning is one of the most overlooked parts of loan applications. Many people borrow at the maximum eligible amount and later struggle with repayment. The better approach is to borrow an amount where EMI fits comfortably.

As a general rule, keep EMI within a safe portion of monthly income so you can still manage rent, groceries, travel, savings, and unexpected expenses.

4) The most common mistakes borrowers make

  • Wrong PAN format (example: missing last character)
  • Incorrect phone number causing unreachable verification
  • Unrealistic loan amount compared to income profile
  • Too many applications in a short period
  • Ignoring EMI affordability and future commitments

5) Responsible borrowing: the NextGen approach

At NextGen Finance, we encourage customers to borrow responsibly. A loan is not just money received — it’s a commitment for repayment. The best loan is one that improves your life without creating pressure on your monthly budget.
